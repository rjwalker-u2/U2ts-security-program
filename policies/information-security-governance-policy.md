1. Purpose

The purpose of this Information Security Governance Policy is to establish the structure, leadership, accountability, and oversight required to manage information security across U2TS.
This policy ensures:

Clear security leadership roles

Governance committees and reporting structures

Consistent accountability

Risk-based decision-making

Alignment between business goals and security strategy

Compliance with regulatory and contractual obligations

This policy forms the foundation of the U2TS Information Security Program (ISP).

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

All U2TS business units

All employees, contractors, vendors, and service providers

All information systems, networks, applications, and cloud environments

All data owned, processed, or stored by U2TS

3. Governance Structure
3.1 Information Security Steering Committee (ISSC)

The ISSC provides oversight and strategic direction for the security program.

Membership:

CISO (Chair)

CTO

Director of Engineering

Head of IT Operations

HR Representative

Legal Counsel

Compliance Manager

Responsibilities:

Approve security strategy and budget

Review risk posture quarterly

Approve policies and major initiatives

Prioritize remediation actions

Review incident trends

Provide executive visibility

Meets: Quarterly or during major incidents.

3.2 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Responsible for:

Security program development and leadership

Policy creation and enforcement

Security budgeting & resource planning

Risk management oversight

Incident response leadership

Compliance and audit readiness

Vendor security review oversight

Reporting to Executive Leadership and Board

The CISO has authority to enforce security requirements across all departments.

3.3 Security Team Responsibilities

Monitor threats and vulnerabilities

Maintain logging and detection systems

Conduct investigations

Perform risk assessments

Review and approve access requests

Conduct security awareness training

Support audits (SOC 2, HIPAA, etc.)

3.4 Department Responsibilities
Engineering

Implement secure SDLC

Patch vulnerabilities

Maintain CI/CD security

IT Operations

Maintain infrastructure security

Enforce access controls

Deploy endpoint protection

HR

Support onboarding/offboarding security workflows

Legal

Manage privacy, breach notification, and compliance requirements

4. Information Security Strategy & Objectives
U2TS security strategy is based on:

Zero Trust principles

Least privilege access

Continuous monitoring

Risk-based decision-making

Compliance with SOC 2 and NIST CSF

Security built into business operations

Annual Objectives:

Improve risk posture

Maintain audit readiness

Reduce incident likelihood and impact

Ensure strong vendor security

Enhance detection & response capabilities

5. Risk Management

U2TS follows a continuous risk management process aligned with NIST 800-37 and ISO 27005.

Risk Management Steps:

Identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, and impacts

Analyze likelihood and severity

Evaluate risks against appetite and tolerance

Treat risks (mitigate, transfer, accept, avoid)

Monitor risks continuously

Report risks quarterly to ISSC

Risk Acceptance

Risk acceptance must be documented and approved by:

CISO

Business Owner

Senior Leadership (if high risk)

6. Policy Management
6.1 Creation & Approval

All policies must be:

Written by Security or relevant business owners

Reviewed by Legal

Approved by ISSC or designated authority

6.2 Review Cycle

Policies must be reviewed annually.

6.3 Distribution

Policies must be accessible to all staff through approved platforms (SharePoint, Confluence, etc.).

7. Compliance, Audits & Assessments
U2TS will maintain readiness for:

SOC 2

Customer security questionnaires

Internal audits

External regulatory audits

Requirements:

Annual internal security audit

Third-party penetration testing

Quarterly vulnerability assessments

Evidence retention for 1–3 years depending on audit needs

8. Vendor & Third-Party Governance

Vendors must be evaluated based on risk and must:

Complete a security questionnaire

Sign DPAs and NDAs

Provide evidence of SOC 2 or equivalent controls

Undergo annual reviews

Restrict access to least privilege

High-risk vendors require CISO approval.

9. Security Metrics & KPIs

The CISO must report the following KPIs to leadership:

Operational KPIs

Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)

Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)

Patch compliance rate

Number of incidents by severity

Governance KPIs

Policy review completion

Risk register updates

Vendor assessments completed

Training KPIs

Security training completion rate

Phishing campaign success rate

10. Security Awareness & Training

All employees must complete:

Annual security awareness training

Quarterly phishing simulations

Role-based security training (IT, Engineering, HR, etc.)

Training completion is mandatory for continued system access.

11. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

Revocation of system access

Disciplinary action

Termination

Legal action (if applicable)

12. Review Cycle

This policy must be reviewed:

Annually

After material changes to business operations

Following major audit findings

After significant security incidents
