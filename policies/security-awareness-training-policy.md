1. Purpose

The purpose of this Security Awareness & Training Policy is to ensure all U2TS personnel understand their responsibilities in protecting company assets, recognizing threats, and complying with security requirements.
This policy establishes a structured, continuous security education program consistent with NIST guidance.

2. Scope

This policy applies to:

All employees

Contractors

Consultants

Interns

Third-party personnel with access to U2TS systems or data

Training applies to all U2TS information systems and cloud services.

3. Security Awareness Program Overview

U2TS maintains a formal Security Awareness & Training Program that includes:

Annual security awareness training

Role-based training for technical teams

Phishing simulations

New hire onboarding training

Policy acknowledgement tracking

Ongoing micro-trainings and alerts

Training metrics and KPIs

Documentation for audit evidence

This program is maintained and governed by the CISO.

4. Training Requirements
4.1 New Hire Training

All new hires must complete security training within 7 days of hire, including:

Overview of the Information Security Program

Acceptable Use Policy

Password & MFA requirements

Data handling rules

Phishing awareness

Reporting procedures for incidents

Access to sensitive systems is not granted until training is completed.

4.2 Annual Security Awareness Training

All personnel must complete annual training covering:

Social engineering

Phishing & business email compromise

Password & MFA best practices (NIST 800-63B)

Data classification & handling

Secure remote work

Incident reporting

Risks related to cloud/SaaS usage

Insider threat awareness

Completion is mandatory for continued system access.

4.3 Ongoing Awareness Activities

U2TS will maintain an ongoing security culture by providing:

Monthly security bulletins

Alerts on emerging threats

Slack/Teams security tips

Quarterly micro-learning modules

4.4 Role-Based Training (RBT)

Personnel with elevated access (Engineering, DevOps, IT, Security) must complete additional training, including:

Secure coding practices

CI/CD & pipeline security

Access control & privilege requirements

Incident response responsibilities

Logging & monitoring expectations

Cloud security principles

This aligns with NIST AT-3 (Role-Based Training).

4.5 Phishing Simulation Program

U2TS will run phishing simulations:

At least quarterly

With targeted campaigns for high-risk roles

Tracking click rates & report rates

Employees who fail simulations must complete remediation training within 10 days.

This aligns with NIST AT-4 (Training Records).

5. Policy Acknowledgement Requirements

All personnel must annually acknowledge:

Acceptable Use Policy

Data Classification Policy

Incident Response Responsibilities

Code of Conduct

Privacy Policies

Acknowledgement records must be stored for at least 3 years.

6. Incident Reporting Training

Employees must be trained to report:

Phishing emails

Suspicious login activity

Lost or stolen devices

Unusual system behavior

Data handling mistakes

Unauthorized access attempts

Reports must be sent to security@u2ts.com
 or via the helpdesk ticketing system.

7. Measuring Program Effectiveness

The CISO must track and report:

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):

Annual training completion rate

Phishing simulation failure rate

Mean time to report suspicious emails

% of employees overdue for training

Role-based training coverage

Repeat offenders requiring remediation training

These metrics will be reviewed during ISSC meetings.

8. Responsibilities
CISO

Oversees program

Reviews KPIs

Approves training content

Security Team

Administers phishing tests

Tracks completion

Provides training materials

Supports audits

Managers

Ensure team compliance

Address training failures

All Employees

Complete assigned training on time

Report suspicious activity

Follow all related policies

9. Enforcement

Failure to comply with training requirements may result in:

Removal of system access

Disciplinary action

Termination

Contract cancellation for third parties

10. Review Cycle

This policy must be reviewed annually or after:

Major incidents

Regulatory changes

Audit findings

Significant organizational changes
